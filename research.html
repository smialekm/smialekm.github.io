Research

Research Group

I am the leader of the Software Modeling Group (SMoG)Institute of Theory of Electrical Engineering, Measurement and Information Systems at the Faculty of Electrical Engineering of the Warsaw University of Technology. The main area of interest of the SMoG group is modeling of software. The group is mostly engaged in research around object-oriented software development methods and their applications. SMoG's area of concern are also CASE tools that try to realize the ideas of MDD (Model-Driven Development) and which enhance the possibility of using UML (Unified Modeling Language) as a universal "lingua franca" of software engineering. SMoG research activities are often related to the field of meta-modeling, such as issues which pertain creation of MOF-based definitions of languages. Other SMoG's research interests include scenario-based requirements engineering, model transformations and software reuse.

Research Projects

BalticLSC (Baltic Large Scale Computing)

I was the Project Coordinator for the BalticLSC project consortium. WUT (Politechnika Warszawska) was the Lead Partner of the consortium of eight institutions from around the Baltic Sea Region. The project was funded from the Interreg BSR scheme of the European Union (project #075). It has started in September 2018 and have succesfully finished in December 2021. More information can be found on the project's web page, and info page.

ReDSeeDS (Requirements Driven Software Development System)

I was the Scientific Coordinator for the ReDSeeDS project consortium. This involved leadership of the consortium of eleven institutions from around Europe. The project was funded from the 6th Framework Programme for Scientific Research of the EU (contract #033596). It has started in September 2006 and finished successfully in August 2009. More information can be found on the project's web page, and info page.

REMICS (REuse and Migration of legacy applications to Interoperable Cloud Services)

I was the leader of the WUT team that took part in the REMICS consortium consisting of twelve institutions from around Europe. The project was funded from the 7th Framework Programme for Scientific Research of the EU (contract #257793). Our involvement has started in October 2011 and finished successfully in September 2013. More information can be found on the info page.

ICONS (Intelligent Content Management System)

I was responsible for the development of methodology within the Infovide team. The project was funded from the 5th Framework Programme for Scientific Reserch of the EU (contract IST-2001-32429 ). The project consortium consisted of seven institutions and took place from January 2002 to April 2004. More information can be found of the info page.
